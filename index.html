Shahinur Hasnat Rahat

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate from Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET) with a strong research focus on composite materials, additive manufacturing, and biomimetic structures. My work centers on understanding structure–property relationships through experimental characterization, materials processing, and emerging data-driven design approaches.

My research experience spans natural fiber-reinforced composites, thermomechanical and fire behavior of polymer composites, and architected material concepts inspired by biological systems. I am particularly interested in how processing parameters and material architecture influence multifunctional performance, with the goal of developing lightweight, efficient, and application-ready materials.

I am currently preparing applications for PhD programs (Fall 2026), seeking to work at the intersection of mechanics, advanced manufacturing, and materials design optimization. I aim to contribute to research on multifunctional materials that combine experimental methods with computational and data-informed strategies.

Publications

The role of sodium silicate on the thermo-mechanical and fire resistance of novel sandwich structures
M. A. I. Shaikot, C. Biswas, Ahammad, S. H. Rahat
Journal of Sandwich Structures and Materials,2025(Published)

→ View Report (PDF)

Developed lightweight sandwich composites with sodium-silicate–bonded hybrid jute/polyester cores and woven jute–epoxy skins, demonstrating that increasing sodium silicate concentration significantly enhances tensile and flexural strength, stiffness, and fire resistance while maintaining low thermal conductivity, making the structures suitable for load-bearing, fire-resistant insulation applications.

Investigation on thermal and fire properties of sodium silicate-treated jute fiber reinforced epoxy composites
S. H. Rahat, Md. Arifuzzaman
In preparation, 2024
pre-print (coming soon)

Examines the effect of sodium-silicate surface treatment concentration on the thermal conductivity, fire resistance, hardness, and impact strength of jute fiber–reinforced epoxy composites fabricated via hand lay-up. The study demonstrates that sodium-silicate treatment significantly alters thermo-mechanical and fire performance, highlighting its role in improving flame resistance and thermal insulation while influencing mechanical properties across different treatment levels.

Design & Manufacturing of a Formula Student Car (Team Kilo Flight)
Designer & Mechanical Control Expert, 2020–2024

As a member of Team Kilo Flight, I contributed to the design, manufacturing, and testing of a single-seater Formula Student race car developed in compliance with the Formula Student UK and Formula SAE rulebooks. The vehicle, Kilo Flight Alpha, underwent comprehensive static and dynamic testing, including design validation, manufacturing review, and performance evaluation for competition readiness. The team participated in Formula Student UK (2020–2022) and Formula SAE (2023) events, gaining hands-on experience in translating engineering design into a functional racing prototype under real-world constraints.
